Data Overview

Figure 1 displays the distribution of all individual anxiety scores across all sounds, while Figure 2 shows the distribution of the average anxiety scores per sound. The overall anxiety score mean is 54/100. Although responses vary widely between participants (standard deviation = 33/100), this variability reflects individual differences in sound perception rather than measurement noise. However, it does not prevent clear differences in anxiety levels between sounds from being observed.

Order Bias & Variance Analysis

Fatigue or Calibration Effect ?

Presentation order of the sounds does not bias average scores (r = 0.009, p = 0.96). However, a significant calibration effect is visible: score dispersion narrows over time (Coeff: -0.084, p < 0.001).

This indicates that participants refined their internal scale, rating less sounds with extreme scores as the experiment progressed.
